Delivering Fonts to Clients

Research Analysis Points about the "Tiny5" Font:

  • Font Style: "Tiny5" appears to be designed for minimalism and simplicity, likely featuring thin strokes and a straightforward design.
  • Legibility: It is optimized for use in small sizes, but its legibility at different sizes should be assessed.
  • Character Support: Research the character support of the font, including special characters, diacritics, and symbols.
  • Usage Context: Investigate the typical contexts where "Tiny5" is used, such as digital interfaces or print media.
  • Designer and Foundry: Research the designer or foundry behind the font to understand its background and reputation.
  • License and Availability: Check the licensing terms and availability of the font, including different weights and styles within the family.
  • User Reviews and Feedback: Look for user reviews and feedback about the font to assess its usability and aesthetic appeal.

This section describes how to deliver a specific font to a client (browser) using the @import rule in CSS.

Steps to Deliver a Font

  1. Choose a Font: Select a font from any source. For example, Google Fonts offers a wide range of fonts.
  2. Import the Font: Use the @import rule in your CSS to import the font: 
                        
@import url('https://fonts.googleapis.com/css2?family=Tiny5&display=swap');

body {
    font-family: 'Tiny5', sans-serif; 
}

By following these steps, the selected font will be applied to the client site.
